Object-oriented design

Flexible OO design

  • Use modules
  • Use interfaces
  • Use abstractions
  • Separate concerns
  • Minimize coupling
Information hiding, or more generally behavior hiding, is the foundation of object-oriented approaches. Delay commitment to the internal design of the module until the requirements of the clients on the interfaces stabilize.
Design is more the art of preserving changeability than it is the act of achieving perfection.
Sandi Metz from Practical Object-Oriented Development in Ruby
