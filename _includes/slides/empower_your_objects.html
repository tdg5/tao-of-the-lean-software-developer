Design good objects and let them do their job!

The ability of a system to achieve its purpose depends on how well the parts work together, not just how well they perform individually.

Teams of objects can be thought of as a layer

Layers provide high cohesion within the layer and a separation of concerns between the layers, two fundamental architectural patterns in software design.

High cohesion

High cohesion is a pattern that attempts to keep objects appropriately focused. Cohesion refers to the degree to which elements of an object or module belong together.

Separation of concerns

For each one concern, other concerns are irrelevant. Separation of concerns is a means of hiding implementation behavior and deferring decision making.

Separation of concerns

Since you can't build complete flexibility into a system, you should try to group things that are likely to change together and hide them from the rest of the system.

Putting things that vary together ... and hiding them from the rest of the system allows changes to be made that have only local impact and do not disrupt huge parts of the system.

Single Responsibility Principle

A class should do the smallest possible useful thing; that is, it should have a single responsibility.

Sandi Metz from Practical Object-Oriented Development in Ruby
