A brief history of Agile

Agile software development found its genesis in dissatisfaction with the ubiquitous "waterfall" model of the 1980s and 90s.

The waterfall model is a sequential design process in which progress is seen as flowing steadily downwards (like a waterfall) through the phases of conception, initiation, analysis, design, construction, testing, implementation and maintenance.

The Emergence of Agile

Recognizing the inefficiencies of waterfall methodology, a group of software engineers from all over the U.S. gathered in February of 2001 to discuss the merits of leaner, more agile development strategies.

The result, a collection of methodologies that would go on to redefine the modern software development landscape:

The Agile Manifesto

Tenets of the Agile Manifesto

We are uncovering better ways of developing software by doing it and helping others do it. Through this work we have come to value:

Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
Working software over comprehensive documentation
Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
Responding to change over following a plan

That is, while there is value in the items on the right, we value the items on the left more.
